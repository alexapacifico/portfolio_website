Abstract

In my senior lab class, I was assigned a team to develop a reusable rocket which will need to launch and then ballistically land close to an offshore recovery platform. The objectives of this project were to estimate the drag and understand the effect of spinning on the flight stability of the rocket, develop a theoretical and empirical model to predict the launch, Experimentally validate the model, and successfully launch the rocket to the given target within the allowable range. The success of the launch was determined at a competition at the end of the semester to see which team could most accurately launch their rocket to a distance given only 10 minutes before the launch time.
